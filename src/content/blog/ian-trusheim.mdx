In early 2024, bassist Ian Trusheim set himself a demanding challenge: one new 
bass line, every day, on <a href="https://www.instagram.com/iantrusheim/" target="_blank">his Instagram account</a>. 
What started as a discipline exercise quickly evolved into a masterclass in 
restraint.

## Groove over excess

Today’s social media landscape is a race to the top. Algorithms reward the 
flashiest, the fastest, and the loudest. Ian Trusheim, however, has chosen 
a different path.

Rather than chasing instant impact, he focuses on space, tone, and 
impeccable timing. His bass lines breathe. They leave room for the music to 
unfold and for other instruments to shine. This deliberate slowness runs 
counter to the digital noise, yet it is precisely what makes his work so 
compelling. By resisting excess, Trusheim reminds us that groove is not about 
virtuosity alone -- it’s about feel and intention.

## The art of simplicity

Simplicity should never be mistaken for ease. Writing a bass line that is 
both minimal and memorable is a rare skill.

At VOLUME OFF, we believe that such lines form the backbone of the most 
enduring tracks in music history. Think of John Deacon’s work on 
“Another One Bites the Dust,” Paul McCartney’s line on “Come Together,” 
or the iconic pulse of Gorillaz’s “Feel Good Inc.” These lines endure not 
because they are complex, but because they are perfectly placed.

## A year-long creative experiment

This philosophy naturally led Ian to his most ambitious project: a challenge 
to create a simple, original two-bar bass line every day for an entire year. 
Posted daily, these short performances became an open creative laboratory.

Some entries are straightforward grooves, while others evolve into fully formed 
miniature compositions, often enhanced by collaborations with drummers like 
<a href="https://www.instagram.com/sluprok" target="_blank">Sluprok</a>. 
Together, they transform a simple idea into a complete musical moment.

## Discipline, growth, and bass monogamy

In a conversation on 
<a href="https://youtu.be/-clvLZZtVg0?si=JVUAcZDsBSeCYJSy" target="_blank">Scott’s Bass Lessons</a>, 
Ian reflects on the breakthroughs that emerge from strict routine. 
He speaks about creative blocks and how consistency leads to greater 
musical confidence.

A key element of his signature warm tone is his iconic 1966 Fender Precision 
Bass, strung with flatwounds. On his own channel, he explores this further in 
a <a href="https://youtu.be/8dARo0u-wlg?si=EY5M02LXgLWHvriV" target="_blank">
video dedicated to “bass monogamy”</a> -— a thoughtful reflection on commitment to 
a single sound and an artistic vision.


## Beyond the challenge: The Moriel Trio

Although the original challenge was conceived as a 365-day journey, Ian chose 
to conclude it on day 211. Rather than a setback, this decision allowed him to 
redirect his energy toward collaborative work, like The Moriel Trio.

Alongside 
<a href="https://www.instagram.com/jazzitdown?utm_source=ig_web_button_share_sheet&igsh=cmdnM3ZzNDZxbHc3" target="_blank">Grant Fisher</a> and 
<a href="https://www.instagram.com/gbone0623?utm_source=ig_web_button_share_sheet&igsh=MTVlYnhtazVkZTJlaA==" target="_blank">Mike Gambone</a>, 
the trio recently released an EP that captures the same values found in 
Ian’s daily lines: groove, restraint, and musical conversation. It’s available 
now on all streaming platforms and Bandcamp.

## Looking ahead

In a landscape driven by immediacy, Ian Trusheim stands out. His work is a 
reminder that music does not need to shout to be heard —- and that sometimes, 
the most powerful ideas are the simplest ones.

**He is exactly the kind of sound we turn 
our Volume Off for.**
