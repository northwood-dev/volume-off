If the name doesn't ring a bell yet, it’s only a matter of time. 
Canadian singer-songwriter and keyboardist Elysia Biro recently stepped into 
the light with her debut single, “Just Wait For a Minute.” It’s more than a 
release; it’s a quiet manifesto for tenderness.

In the refrain, she admits: “Just wait a minute, 'cause my heart is full 
to the limit.” This isn't a plea for distance, but an honest confession of 
emotional saturation. In a world of over-produced noise, Biro celebrates love 
in its simplest form. Her vocals don't fight for attention; they invite you 
to slow down and breathe with her.

## From Vancouver Island to the World

The music video, filmed amidst the serene Canadian pine forests, feels like a 
homecoming. It’s a nod to Vancouver Island, where Elysia’s journey began.

Her background is formidable: piano at age four, jazz explorations by thirteen, 
followed by a Master’s degree in Music and a diploma in classical piano. 
This academic rigour hasn't stifled her; it has given her the tools to be 
effortlessly creative. She bridges the gap between the technical precision of 
a scholar and the raw curiosity of a songwriter.

## Transparency in the Process

Before this official debut, Biro built a sanctuary for music lovers online. 
By sharing jazz transcriptions, piano sketches, and raw performances, 
she deconstructed the "mystery" of the artist. This transparency—showing the 
work behind the magic—is what makes her connection with her

## The Future: A Modern Fusion

Signed to a joint deal with Universal Music Canada and Deutsche Grammophon, 
Elysia Biro sits at a fascinating crossroads.

As she sings, “I’m honing into something,” she isn't just talking about 
a relationship; she’s describing her own artistic trajectory. Drawing lines 
between the legends (Oscar Peterson, Ella Fitzgerald) and the new guard 
(Laufey, Olivia Dean), she is carving out a distinctive voice that feels 
both timeless and necessary.

At VOLUME OFF, we’re turning the noise down to let her piano breathe. 
She is, without a doubt, the kind of sound we’ve been waiting for.
